Thank God! In the dark, bright people are very visible. In His Sermon on the Mount, Christ called his disciples and followers to be a light to the world. At this difficult time, it is important for our country to receive help and support from sincere people and those who care about Ukraine. On behalf of TOBO "Heart of Mercy" we express our sincere gratitude to the sponsors and partners of PRD. We are pleased to understand that in this trouble we are not alone, but you are with us in your prayers and material support. We directed our work in several directions: 1. Providing assistance to people affected by hostilities personally 2. Cooperation with churches and other public organizations to help and support refugees from military aggression. For many years, our charity has been providing humanitarian aid to socially vulnerable groups in Ukraine. This experience contributed to the establishment of mechanisms in these difficult times of war. Several testimonies about the people we met and helped as much as possible: 1. Eugene, a resident of Kharkiv in the past participated in the anti-terrorist operation, living in Kharkov had a car, house, apartment, his wife had his own dentistry. In a few days he lost everything, got by evacuation train to Ternopil and was at the refugee reception point on the street. Textile, all he had left was the clothes he was wearing (sports pants, torn sneakers, a thin jacket) and a small backpack with documents without finances for subsistence, he had only 200 hryvnias. And the opportunity to be at the refugee reception center 3 days. What was done: Clothes were provided: new sneakers (autumn-winter), winter jacket, new jeans, personal hygiene products (toothbrush, toothpaste, deodorant, shaving products), financial assistance for travel to Lviv with further arrival to Novovolynsk, Volyn region. In a few days he had the opportunity to go abroad where he met his wife and will build a new life without war. 2. Many people responded to help for IDPs and were ready to receive them in their homes. Since most migrants do not have the finances for their livelihood, buying food, clothing, etc. decided to support them with food kits that included basic foods (rice, pasta, cereals, stews, flour, sugar, etc.). 3. Together with the Local Church "Druzhba" in Ternopil, assistance was provided in the reception, nutrition, spiritual and psychological support of people affected by the war. Every night, 50 to 200 refugees are received at the House of Prayer. 4.. Together with the Church in Zbarazh, material assistance was provided to more than 40 migrants from Mariupol who left the blockade. Clothes and food were provided, a house was rented, cosmetic repairs were made to improve living conditions, mattresses and utensils were provided for comfortable living.
